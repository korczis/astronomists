## THE 5 PILLARS OF TONE (How We Sound)

| Pillar | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Epistemic Clarity** | No hedging without evidence | "78% confident because 7/8 sources agree" NOT "seems to suggest" |
| **Intellectual Rigor** | Refuse buzzwords, use precise language | "127+ OSINT adapters" NOT "AI-powered intelligence" |
| **Operational Honesty** | Concrete outcomes, measured impact | "60–80% faster than manual assessment" NOT "improves decision-making" |
| **Philosophical Depth** | Ask hard questions. Position as moral work. | "What are we teaching with this decision?" |
| **Accessible Confidence** | Peer collaborators, empower through "you" | "You have good instincts. Let's verify them with evidence." |

## GOVERNANCE & BRAND CONTROL

### Approved Claims (All Evidence-Backed)
✓ "127+ OSINT sources with source attribution"  
✓ "2-day due diligence vs. 6-week manual process"  
✓ "60–80% faster risk assessment"  
✓ "Every score traces to source evidence"

### Claim Validation
- Every quantitative claim must have documented evidence
- "Faster" claims require methodology + customer data proof
- "Most" or "best" claims require specific evidence
- Grey-zone claims require executive approval

### Tone Enforcement
- All copy passes 5-pillar checklist
- Persona targeting validated (right message to right audience)
- Weekly brand check (team sync, 30 minutes)

### Escalation Path
1. **Quick fixes** (typos, clarity) → Self-correct + log
2. **Tone issues** (buzzword detected) → Rewrite + peer review
3. **Claim issues** (overclaimed, no evidence) → Pull asset + correct + notify
4. **Crisis** (competitor attack, customer complaint) → Use crisis template + notify exec
