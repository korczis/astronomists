## II. TONE OF VOICE GUIDE

### Five Pillars (How We Sound)

#### 1. **Epistemic Clarity** (Sharp, Unambiguous)
- We avoid hedging without evidence ("seems to suggest" is forbidden)
- We name assumptions explicitly ("We assume X because...")
- We distinguish confidence from certainty ("80% confident ≠ certain")
- We never claim what we cannot demonstrate

**Examples**:
- ✓ "This finding is based on 7 sources; confidence is 78% due to one unverified outlier."
- ✗ "It appears that this might possibly be the case, if circumstances permit."

---

#### 2. **Intellectual Rigor** (No Hype, No Shortcuts)
- We refuse marketing buzzwords: "AI-powered," "revolutionary," "next-gen," "unlock insights"
- We use precise technical language that intelligence professionals recognize
- We cite evidence, not adjectives
- We admit unknowns openly

**Examples**:
- ✓ "Our OSINT adapters surface 127+ data sources with attribution chains you can audit."
- ✗ "Harness the power of next-generation intelligence at your fingertips."

---

#### 3. **Operational Honesty** (What It Actually Does)
- We describe concrete outcomes: "Reduces due diligence cycle from 6 weeks to 2 days"
- We specify the problem we solve: "For those uncertain about external risks..."
- We avoid vague benefits ("improves decision-making" = meaningless)
- We measure impact with numbers, not feelings

**Examples**:
- ✓ "60–80% faster risk assessment, with every score traceable to source evidence."
- ✗ "Empowers smarter choices across your organization."

---

#### 4. **Philosophical Depth** (Why It Matters)
- We ask the hard questions our users face ("What are we teaching with this decision?")
- We position intelligence work as moral: "Integrity in investigation is sacred"
- We speak to legacy and stewardship ("We build for those who inherit our decisions")
- We acknowledge the stakes: "Getting this right affects people"

**Examples**:
- ✓ "Every recommendation is traceable to evidence. Every decision is auditable. This is how you earn trust."
- ✗ "Make better decisions faster."

---

#### 5. **Accessible Confidence** (Credible but Not Arrogant)
- We speak as peer collaborators, not saviors
- We use "we" (inclusive) and "you" (empowering) frequently
- We celebrate the user's wisdom ("You know your industry. We help you verify it.")
- We avoid false modesty ("We're still learning") AND false certainty ("We're perfect")

**Examples**:
- ✓ "You already have intuition about this market. Let's verify it with evidence."
- ✗ "Trust us completely" OR "We're just a tool, make your own decisions"

## XI. CRISIS & CORRECTION MESSAGING

### If a Claim Is Found Inaccurate

**Process**:
1. **Immediate**: Notify all teams (marketing, sales, executive)
2. **24 hours**: Correct all live marketing (website, social, collateral)
3. **48 hours**: Issue transparent correction statement
4. **Follow-up**: Customer communication if claim was in pitch

**Example Correction Statement**:
> "We initially stated that [claim]. Investigation reveals [reality]. We've corrected this 
> across our marketing materials and informed all customers. This reflects our commitment: 
> the same rigor we apply to intelligence, we apply to ourselves."

---

### If a Customer Challenges Our Evidence

**Response Playbook**:
1. **Listen**: Gather full context (what evidence, what use case, what doubt)
2. **Investigate**: Review the specific case (was methodology applied correctly?)
3. **Respond transparently**: 
   - If we were right, show the evidence chain
   - If we were wrong, acknowledge it + explain what we learned
   - If it's a known limitation, say so + explain the constraint

**Example Response**:
> "You're right that our OSINT adapter missed [source]. Here's why [technical reason]. 
> We've updated the adapter + alerted all users of the gap. This is the intelligence work."
